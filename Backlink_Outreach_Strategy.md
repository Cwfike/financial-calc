# Backlink Outreach Strategy — financial-calc.com

**Goal:** Acquire 20–30 quality backlinks within 90 days to start building domain authority.

---

## Why Backlinks Matter Right Now

You have 8 indexed pages and no referring domains. Google treats new sites with zero backlinks as unproven — even great content ranks poorly without some initial authority signal. You don't need hundreds of links. A handful from credible personal finance sites moves the needle meaningfully.

---

Submit to tool/calculator directories. These are low-effort and provide immediate do-follow links.

## Tier 2 — Reddit & Communities (Week 1–2, ~3 hours)

Reddit has strict no-spam rules. The key is to contribute genuinely first, then share your tool when it's directly relevant.

### Target Subreddits
- **r/personalfinance** (17M members) — Answer questions about mortgage rates, auto loans. When someone asks "how do I know what rate I'll get?", share the calculator.
- **r/FirstTimeHomeBuyer** — Very active; questions about mortgage calculations come up constantly.
- **r/povertyfinance** — Credit score improvement and loan questions are common here.
- **r/askcarsales** — Auto loan questions; share the auto loan calculator when directly relevant.
- **r/debtfree** — Credit card payoff and debt consolidation questions.

### Rules
- Never post "check out my calculator" cold — you'll get banned.
- Spend 1–2 weeks genuinely answering questions in each subreddit first.
- Only share the link when it directly answers a specific question someone asked.
- Example: User asks "I have a 680 credit score, what mortgage rate can I expect?" → Answer the question fully, then add "I built a calculator that shows exact rates by credit score if you want to plug in your numbers."

## Tier 3 — Personal Finance Bloggers (Weeks 2–4, ~5 hours)

Find bloggers who write about mortgages, auto loans, or credit scores and pitch your calculator as a resource they can link to.

### How to Find Targets
Search Google for:
- `"mortgage calculator" site:wordpress.com`
- `"credit score mortgage rate" inurl:blog`
- `"best financial calculators" 2024 OR 2025`
- `"auto loan calculator review"`

Look for sites with 10K–100K monthly visitors (check with Ahrefs free or SimilarWeb). Too small = no value. Too big = won't respond.

### Outreach Email Template

```
Subject: Free resource for your readers — mortgage calculator with credit score rates

Hi [Name],

I came across your article on [specific article title] — really useful breakdown of [specific thing they covered].

I recently built a free mortgage calculator that does something I haven't seen elsewhere: it adjusts interest rates based on the user's credit score range, so readers get a more accurate picture of their actual monthly payment instead of a generic estimate.

If you ever update [their article] or write about mortgage tools, it might be a useful resource to mention. Happy to share more details if helpful.

[Your name]
financial-calc.com/calculators/mortgage
```

**Key tips:**
- Personalize every email — mention their specific article
- Send 5–10 per week, not 50 at once
- Follow up once after 1 week if no reply
- Track who you've contacted in a spreadsheet

## Tier 4 — Guest Posts (Month 2–3, ~10 hours)

Write one solid article for another personal finance site in exchange for a backlink. This takes time but produces the highest-quality links.

### Target Publications
- **Money Under 30** (moneyunder30.com) — accepts contributor pitches
- **The College Investor** (thecollegeinvestor.com) — accepts guest posts on financial topics
- **Millennial Money** — open to contributors
- **Medium** (medium.com) — publish under your own profile, link back to site

### Topic Pitches That Work Well
1. "What Credit Score Do You Actually Need to Buy a House in 2026?" (with data from your calculator)
2. "I Calculated How Much Bad Credit Costs Over a Car Loan — Here's What I Found"
3. "The True Cost of Minimum Credit Card Payments: A Month-by-Month Breakdown"
